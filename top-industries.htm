Top Industries

New Growth

International Isotopes, Inc (INIS): In March 2009, INIS announced their decision to locate a Depleted Uranium Deconversion and Fluorine Extraction Facility in Lea County.  Their products can be used in petroleum operations, photovoltaic solar cells, and refrigerant and aluminum production. Construction on the facility is expected to begin in early 2013. This $115 Million facility will employ between 125-150 full-time employees plus additional construction jobs. www.intisoid.com

Joule Unlimited: Joule is the pioneer of Liquid Fuel from the Sun, enabling direct, continuous conversion of sunlight and waste CO2 to infrastructure-ready fuels and chemicals at virtually unlimited scale and stable, competitive costs. The phase 1 small scale commercial facility is already in production west of Hobbs. Phase 1 will employ 20 people and could increase to 100 during phase 2. www.jouleunlimited.com

Intercontinental Potash Corporation (ICP): A world-class potash find has been identified in Lea County. This potash formation has the potential to produce over 340 million tons of polyhalite, to be mined over a 75-year period. Polyhalite is used as a fertilizer and has low or no chlorides, which makes it highly valuable for use on crops that have a low tolerance for chlorides. Currently preparing their environmental impact analysis, ICP anticipates an operational mine by 2014/2015. When at full scale operations, the $1 Billion ICP mine could employ up to 450 full time employees. www.icpotash.com

United Airlines: Daily flights between Hobbs and Houston started in July 2011 via United ExpressJet. These flights are within a 90 minute connection time of over 200 world-wide destinations. The county and airport management are currently exploring additional flight service options to Albuquerque and other westward locations. www.flyhobbs.com

Exelon/ Wild Cat Wind: This wind project completed a $54 million, 24 MW wind turbine array. The power will feed into Lea County Electric Cooperative’s power grid.

Eldorado Bio-Fuels: Eldorado Bio-Fuels is a leading company in the areas of industrial waste water treatment, algae cultivation and bio-fuel production.  This Santa Fe headquartered company has partnerships with the National Alliance for Advanced Bio-fuels and Bio-products, the Los Alamos National Labs, VM Technologies and other private and commercial oil and gas companies. Eldorado has a demonstration facility in the EnergyPlex and hopes to commercialize their production in the near future.  www.eldoradobiofuels.com

AeonClad: Through a partnership between Emergent Technologies, Inc. and Lea County, a new project is nearly ready to begin operations in Lea County. AeonClad coats feathers to make them water resistant and will be used in lining for cold weather gear. This is just the first of the “research to commercialization” projects that have been realized from the County/ETI partnership.

URENCO USA National Enrichment Facility: URENCO USA is a nuclear-related facility that enriches uranium to be used as fuel in nuclear power plants all over the world. In June, 2010, URENCO USA commenced operations of their uranium enrichment facility. They will continue construction until they reach their target capacity of 5.7 million SWU. When completed, this facility will supply 50% of the nation’s enriched uranium needs. This is a multi-billion dollar project that employs approximately 350 employees and supports between 600-900 ongoing construction jobs. www.nefnm.com

SunEdison: In December 2011, SunEdison completed work on a 50MW photovoltaic solar deployment in Lea and Eddy Counties. Each of the 5 sites has a 10 MW solar array, with four sites in Lea County and one site in Eddy County. The solar arrays will generate enough energy to power more than 192,000 homes for a year. This will be one of the largest photovoltaic projects in North America and has put Lea County on the World’s solar map. www.sunedison.com

Petroleum Industry Expansions: Due to advances in hydrofracking and horizontal drilling, the Permian Basin is experiencing a resurgence of petroleum activities. Several companies, including international manufacturers are exploring the potential of locating new or expanding existing facilities in the EnergyPlex.

New Housing Developments: Ledgestone Apartments, Western States Development, Windscape Apartment Expansion, Gold Creek homes

New Hotels: Holiday Inn Express (nearly completed), Wyndham – Baymont Hotel (under construction), Marriot TowneHome Extended Stay (under construction), Hilton Garden Suites Hotel (announced)

New Retail: Lowe’s, Hibbit Sports, Tractor Supply, Schlotzsky’s, Dollar General, Eagle Theater (expansion), Sterling, Gebo’s, Steele, Denny’s
